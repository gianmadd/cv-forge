# Probe banks — early-career and career changers

Reach for these when calibration flags an **early-career** or **career-changer** path.
People with the least formal experience need *more* probing to surface value, not a
dismissive "keep it short." Don't create a separate branch — shift emphasis using these
probes. Adapt the wording to the user's actual field; these are prompts for you, not a
script to read verbatim. **Probe once, then move on**: raise each topic a single time and
drop it if there's nothing there.

## Early-career / limited formal work history

- Studies, coursework, a capstone or thesis, academic projects.
- Internships, placements, apprenticeships, work experience.
- Part-time, seasonal, or casual jobs — and the transferable skills in them.
- Volunteering, community roles, clubs, societies, sports teams (leadership, reliability).
- Personal or side projects; self-directed learning; things they built or organised.
- Certifications, licences, or training in progress.

## Career changers

- The **transferable skills** from the previous field that carry into the new one.
- The **transition rationale** — why the change, framed as direction, not apology.
- Achievements from the prior career that demonstrate transferable capability.
- Recent study, retraining, projects, or volunteering bridging into the new field.
